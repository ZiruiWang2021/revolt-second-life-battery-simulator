# Supply Chain Strategy

## Objective

ReVolt uses second-life EV battery packs to lower residential storage cost while extending the useful life of battery materials. The supply chain strategy is built around predictable sourcing, safe diagnostics, transparent grading, and responsible end-of-life routing.

## Supply Chain Flow

1. Source retired EV packs from fleet operators, dealerships, dismantlers, insurers, and OEM recycling partners.
2. Screen packs for chemistry, provenance, safety recalls, physical damage, and prior service conditions.
3. Disassemble into pack, module, or cell-level units depending on product architecture and safety requirements.
4. Test remaining capacity, internal resistance, thermal behavior, and self-discharge.
5. Grade modules into residential storage classes:
   - Grade A: primary residential storage.
   - Grade B: backup-heavy or lower-power applications.
   - Reject: recycle or return to qualified materials recovery.
6. Integrate battery management system controls, enclosure, inverter interface, and safety disconnects.
7. Deploy with installation records, warranty terms, and traceable asset IDs.
8. Monitor field performance and route degraded units to repair, redeployment, or recycling.

## Key Partners

| partner type | role |
| --- | --- |
| EV fleet operators | Predictable supply of packs with better service records. |
| Dismantlers and recyclers | Pack recovery, logistics, safety handling, recycling fallback. |
| Test labs | Capacity, safety, and certification support. |
| Installers | Site assessment, electrical work, commissioning. |
| Community energy programs | Low-income household deployment and subsidy coordination. |
| Utilities | Time-of-use tariffs, demand response, resilience programs. |

## Quality Gates

- Verify pack identity, chemistry, and recall status.
- Reject physically damaged, water-exposed, or thermally stressed packs.
- Require minimum remaining capacity threshold for each product class.
- Record module-level test results before integration.
- Validate BMS communication, isolation resistance, temperature sensors, and disconnect behavior.
- Maintain serial-level traceability from incoming pack to deployed system.

## Risk Register

| risk | mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Unpredictable supply volume | Use multiple sourcing channels and prioritize fleet contracts. |
| Inconsistent pack formats | Standardize around a small number of module families first. |
| Safety or warranty exposure | Use conservative grading, certified enclosures, and clear warranty boundaries. |
| Logistics cost | Regionalize testing and integration near pack supply. |
| Regulatory changes | Track battery transport, storage, and recycling rules by market. |
| Customer trust | Publish testing method, warranty terms, and recycling pathway. |

## Business Implications

Second-life packs can improve affordability, but only if testing, logistics, integration, and warranty costs stay below the savings versus new battery systems. The simulator exposes this through installed cost per kWh, fixed installation cost, incentive level, cycle life, and backup reserve requirements.
